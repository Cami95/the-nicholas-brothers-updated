Where did these talented brothers come from?

  • Fayard Antonio Nicholas was born in Mobile, Alabama on October 20th, 1914
  • Harlod Lloyd Nicholas was born in Winston-Salem, Norht Carolina on March 17th, 1921

"One day at the Standard Theater in Philadelphia, I looked onstage and I thought, 'They're having fun up there; I'd like to do something like that.' We worked up an act called 'The Nicholas Kids,' and did it in the living room."- Fayard Nicholas

"When we were doing routines, it was like a love affair."

"We can't do those routines any more; we don't want to, and I'll tell you why--it hurts!"

"My brother and I used our whole bodies, our hands, our personalities and everything. We tried to make it classic. We called our type of dancing classical tap and we just hoped the audience liked it."

"Our father said, "When you're dancing, don't look at your feet, look at the audience. You're not entertaining yourself, you're entertaining the audience"."

They grew up in Philadelphia and were the sons of two musicians who played in their own band at the old standard theater. Their mother played piano while their father played the drums. From a young age, the brothers where exposed to alot of Vaudeville acts but they specificly showed interest in the dancers. These acts included notable talents such as Alice Whitman, Willie Bryant and Bill Robinson.Even with this being the case, they never had an formal dance training.
